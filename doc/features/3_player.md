# 3. Player & Character Systems

## Movement & Exploration
- Tile-based grid; each turn allows one movement or action, processed server-side to avoid cheating.
- Regions include forests, mountains, rivers, cities, castles; context-sensitive encounters per biome.
- Spawn points configured for factions/cities; respawn timers after defeat.

## Combat
- PvE: scripted monster/NPC encounters with aggro ranges; loot tables per creature.
- PvP: duels, party skirmishes, arena tournaments with ranking ladders and rewards.
- Turn-based sequencing with initiative stats; combat log records damage, buffs, debuffs for later review.
- Support group battles with role visibility, spectators, and moderation overrides.

## Progression & Stats
- XP and leveling curve; stat points allocated each level (strength, agility, intellect, stamina, spirit, etc.).
- Reputation and faction alignment influence access to quests, gear, city services.
- Skill trees per class with unlockable abilities via quests/progression milestones.

## Classes & Abilities
- Core classes: Warrior, Mage, Hunter, Priest, Thief; each has unique active/passive skills.
- Class-specific equipment restrictions, resources (rage, mana, energy), and combo mechanics.
- Unlock path for secondary specializations or advanced classes via questlines.

## Items & Inventory
- Equipment slots: weapon (main/off-hand), armor (head/chest/legs/hands/feet), accessories, consumables.
- Inventory management with weight/slot limits; stackable consumables and crafting mats.
- Premium artifacts purchasable with real money but balanced to avoid pay-to-win.
- Enhancement/enchantment system to upgrade gear; risk mechanics (success/failure) tied to crafting skills.

## Crafting & Professions (Character View)
- Gathering skills feed crafting professions; proficiency increases success rates and unlocks recipes.
- Doctor profession handles trauma/healing after fights, enabling shorter downtime for groups.
